## The Blocker — Detail

**File**: `.worktrees/broad-breadth-study/data/breadth_study_1b/daily_breadth.csv`
**Producer**: prior recovery-sweep code (`backtest/breadth_study/recovery_sweep.py` or its pipeline). Not generated by v1.2 work.
**Rows**: 1339 (2021-02-01 → 2026-04-28)
**`breadth_50` first-valid → last-valid window**: 2021-04-13 → 2026-04-28 (1290 rows, ~5 calendar years)
**NaN inside that window**: 23

**The 23 NaN dates (verified — all US market holidays)**:

| Year | Holidays |
|------|----------|
| 2022 | Memorial Day (5/30), Independence Day (7/4), Labor Day (9/5), Thanksgiving (11/24) |
| 2023 | MLK (1/16), Presidents (2/20), Memorial (5/29), Juneteenth (6/19), July 4 (7/4), Labor (9/4), Thanksgiving (11/23) |
| 2024 | MLK (1/15), Presidents (2/19), Memorial (5/27), Juneteenth (6/19), July 4 (7/4), Labor (9/2), Thanksgiving (11/28) |
| 2025 | Carter funeral closure (1/9), MLK/Inauguration (1/20), Presidents (2/17), Memorial (5/26), Juneteenth (6/19) |

**Why it kills the signal**: `backtest/breadth_study/percentile_signal.py` builds
```
signal = breadth.rolling(252, min_periods=252).rank(pct=True).rolling(5, min_periods=5).mean()
```
Any NaN inside the 252-day window propagates NaN through the rank. With 23 mid-stream NaN spread across the sample, the signal is non-NaN on only 31 days for `breadth_50` and 0 days for `breadth_50_active`.

**Plan-level effective-sample expectation**: ~3.8 years. **Observed in stale Task 14 output**: 0.24 years. The third fork correctly refused to commit a `REJECT_NO_SIGNAL` verdict that was driven by data-quality, not by the factor.
